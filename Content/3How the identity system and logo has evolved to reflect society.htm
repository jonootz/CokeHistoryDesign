Slogans

They change quite frequently to go along with the times. Some of the notable that really stand the test of time are ones like 1963's "Things go better with Coke.", 1970's "It's the real thing.", 1982's "Coke is it!", 1985's "America's real choice.", 1986's "Red, white & you." (for Coca-Cola Classic), 1986's "Catch the wave." (for New Coke), 1987's "You can't beat the feeling!.", 1990's "Can't Beat The Real Thing.", 1993's "Always Coca-Cola.", 2000's "Enjoy", 2009's "Open Happiness", and 2014's "Share A Coke". A few notable ones that actual were included in the logo were "Drink", "Enjoy", and "Always". The overall theme here is obvious that Coke is all about happiness, good times and sharing. These are wonderful messages and really affect people from all around the world.

The cola wars

For the first time in its history, Coca Cola was no longer the most popular soft drink. By this point Pepsi's popularity and sales had surpassed their own and the company was in full damage control mode. "One Brand" is a refresh for 's branding. They are combining all related "Coke" products under the umbrella as far as branding goes. For instance the original formula (the flagship product) is now displayed horizontally across the can like it has always has been on the bottles. The traditional logo script spans across the can once taking up the top half of the can and below it is the white dynamic wave. The other three main "Coke" related products: Life, Zero, and Diet Coke follow the same design, all now just display on the upper half of the can just like the flagship except Diet Coke which displays Diet Coke but now in the same to match better. Each also have the wave placed the same way. This really ties the four products together. Each can does have a unique color and accent color that they retain from previous designs. The Life's can is green and the accent color, that includes the wave color and font color are both white just like regular Coke's. The Zero's can is black with accent color being red, and Diet Coke in which the can is silver and red as the accent color with the exception of "Diet" which has been black for many years now. Below the main logo and above the wave in much smaller font are descriptions that identify the differences of each product and also displayed is the unique names: Life, zero which appear as subtitles in a way which is a powerful way to signify that they are really part of the family.

The astute amongst you may notice the lack of a registration symbol on this logo. At this point in its lifespan, the brand had not yet been registered at the patent office.
