Bottling pre 1915

In 1894 Mississippi shop owner Joseph A. Biedenharn was impressed by Coca‑Cola that he bottled it in what was a common glass bottle at the time called a Hutchinson and sent a case to Asa Griggs Candler. Candler was polite and thanked him, but never accepted Biedenham's offer to bottle the drink because he thought bottling would not take off (Journey Staff, "Contour Bottle History")

 

Bottling was a challange because of the success of there were now imitators and lack of packaging consistency among the 1,000 bottling plants at the time, the bottlers agreed that a distinctive beverage needed a standard and distinctive bottle (Coca-Cola History)
